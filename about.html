About Me
I got my undergrad degree in Biochemistry from University of Bristol, an MSc in Bioinformatics and Theoretical Systems Biology from Imperial College London, and finally my PhD in Structural Biology from Birkbeck College, University of London.
I've been a postdoctoral researcher at Queen Mary University, University of London since 2017. I develop and maintain a number of websites and tools that serve the structural biology community, with a particular focus on Circular Dichroism Spectroscopy. Occasionally I will detach myself from my computer and go into a wet-lab to collect data.
If you want to contact me, here is my LinkedIn.
A few of my research interests are:
  • Exploring how much structural information can be extracted from circular dichroism data
  • Integrating circular dichroism data with computational techniques like molecular dynamics simulation
  • the creation of new methods for quality control of protein samples (e.g. in biosimilar development)
I have picked up a number of computer languages over the years, including:
  • Python (including Numpy/Scipy/Pandas, Tensorflow, scikit-learn)
  • Javascript (including React)
  • PHP
  • Perl
  • R and Matlab
  • HTML/CSS
Outside of work, I like to take on projects to learn new skills - for example, creating small web tools that make hard tasks easy for undergraduate students I supervise, or visualising interesting data I find. Of course, I have a life too - I enjoy bouldering and jumpe rope and have recently got into carving wooden spoons... perhaps having "a life" is being a little charitable...
